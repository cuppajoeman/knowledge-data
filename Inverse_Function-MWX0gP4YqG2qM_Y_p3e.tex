\begin{defn*}{Inverse Function}
Inverse Function: Let $f : A \to B$ be a function. The inverse of $f$ is another function, written: 
\[
	f^{-1} : B \to A
\] defined by


\[
	\forall x \in A, \forall y \in B, x = f^{-1}\left(y\right) \Leftrightarrow y = f\left(x\right)
\]

Assuming that $f^{-1}$ is a function.

\subsubsection*{Equivalent Definition}
\[
	\begin{cases} \forall x \in A, & f^{-1}\left(f\left(x\right)\right)=x\\ \forall y \in B, & f\left(f^{-1}\left(y\right)\right)=y \end{cases}
\]
\end{defn*}
